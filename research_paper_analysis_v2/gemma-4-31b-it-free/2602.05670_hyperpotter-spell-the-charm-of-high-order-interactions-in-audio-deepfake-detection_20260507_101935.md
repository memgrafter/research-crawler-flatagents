# HyperPotter: Spell the Charm of High-Order Interactions in Audio Deepfake Detection

## Quick Facts
* arXiv ID: 2602.05670
* Source URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.05670
* Reference Count: 40
* Primary Result: Achieved a 22.15% average relative gain over baseline and 13.96% over SOTA across four challenging cross-domain datasets.

## Executive Summary
HyperPotter is a novel framework for audio deepfake detection (ADD) designed to overcome the limitations of models that rely solely on local or pairwise feature relations. By leveraging O-information theory, the authors demonstrate that "synergistic interactions"—where discriminative artifacts only appear when multiple features are analyzed jointly—are essential for robust generalization across diverse spoofing attacks.

The system implements a prototype-oriented hypergraph neural network (HGNN) coupled with a relational artifact amplification module. This approach allows the model to capture high-order interactions (HOIs) and maintain long-term memory of spoofing patterns via class-aware prototype banks, significantly improving detection performance in cross-scenario environments.

## Method Summary
HyperPotter shifts from pairwise graph modeling to high-order interaction modeling using a hypergraph structure. It employs clustering-based hyperedges to group related features and a relational artifact amplification module to sharpen the distinction between genuine and spoofed audio.

To ensure stability and efficiency, the framework integrates class-aware prototype banks. These banks serve as anchors for hyperedge initialization and alignment, allowing the model to maintain consistent representations of deepfake artifacts across different datasets.

## Key Results
* Average relative performance gain of 22.15% over the baseline model.
* Significant EER reductions on key benchmarks: In-the-Wild (5.72%), ASVspoof 2021 DF (1.78%), and FoR (3.89%).
* Outperformed state-of-the-art (SOTA) methods by 13.96% on four high-difficulty cross-domain datasets.

## Why This Works (Mechanism)
The framework operates on the principle of synergy identified via O-information theory. Unlike redundancy (where information is repeated) or independence, synergy occurs when the joint state of multiple variables provides more information than the sum of their parts. In ADD, certain spoofing artifacts are not visible in individual frequency bins or pairs but emerge as patterns across high-order feature sets. HyperPotter explicitly models these interactions via hyperedges, which can connect any number of nodes, capturing the complex "synergy" of deepfake artifacts.

## Foundational Learning
* O-Information Theory: Needed to quantify synergistic vs. redundant interactions; Quick check: Does the joint entropy of features reveal more than pairwise sums?
* Hypergraph Neural Networks: Needed to model relations between more than two nodes; Quick check: Are hyperedges dynamically updated or static?
* Prototype Learning: Needed for cross-domain alignment and memory; Quick check: Do prototype banks converge across different datasets?
* Equal Error Rate (EER): Needed to evaluate binary classification performance in spoofing; Quick check: Is the EER consistent across varied noise levels?

## Architecture Onboarding
Component Map: Input Audio -> Feature Extraction -> Prototype Bank Alignment -> Hyperedge Clustering -> Hypergraph Convolution -> Relational Artifact Amplification -> Classification

Critical Path: The flow from prototype-based hyperedge initialization to the high-order interaction convolution, as this is where synergistic artifacts are amplified.

Design Tradeoffs: High-order modeling increases representation power and generalization but introduces higher computational complexity compared to standard GCNs or MLPs.

Failure Signatures: Performance collapse in the presence of adversarial perturbations targeting feature synergy or high latency during the hypergraph construction phase.

First Experiments:
1. Validate O-information synergy levels on a single dataset to confirm the presence of high-order artifacts.
2. Compare EER with and without the prototype banks to measure the impact on long-term memory.
3. Test the impact of varying the number of nodes per hyperedge on detection accuracy.

## Limitations
* Computational overhead of hypergraph construction and prototype bank maintenance may hinder real-time inference.
* Theoretical uncertainty regarding whether "synergistic interactions" correlate to physical codec flaws or are latent space emergent patterns.
* Sensitivity of clustering-based hyperedge initialization remains unquantified.

## Confidence
* Cross-Dataset Performance Gain: High
* HOI Superiority over Pairwise Relations: Medium
* Generalizability to Future Attack Vectors: Medium

## Next Checks
1. Latency Analysis: Quantify the inference time increase compared to the baseline to evaluate deployment viability.
2. Ablation of Prototype Banks: Measure the specific performance decay when removing prototype banks to verify their role in memory.
3. Adversarial Robustness Test: Evaluate the framework against targeted perturbations designed to disrupt high-order feature synergy.
