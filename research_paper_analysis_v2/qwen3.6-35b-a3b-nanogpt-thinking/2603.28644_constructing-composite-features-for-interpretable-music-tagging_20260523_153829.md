# Constructing Composite Features for Interpretable Music-Tagging

## Quick Facts
- **arXiv ID**: 2603.28644
- **Source URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.28644
- **Reference count**: 0
- **Primary result**: Evolved composite features augmented XGBoost classifiers, improving MTG-Jamendo ROC-AUC by 0.002–0.005 and GTZAN accuracy by 0.035–0.050 while yielding human-readable feature formulas.

## Executive Summary
Deep music-tagging models frequently rely on opaque neural architectures that obscure how acoustic signals map to semantic labels. This work introduces a Genetic Programming (GP) pipeline that automatically evolves composite features by mathematically combining established audio descriptors. By treating feature construction as a direct, fitness-driven optimization problem, the method discovers non-linear, task-aligned synergies that static feature sets typically miss. The approach demonstrates that interpretable, formulaic features can significantly boost classifier performance while remaining transparent enough to audit signal-perceptual interactions.

## Method Summary
The pipeline extracts E23 signal-level and ALL62 harmonic/perceptual descriptors using Essentia and Omnizart, applying zero-mean/unit-variance standardization. A DEAP-based Genetic Programming module evolves symbolic expression trees using arithmetic, trigonometric, conditional, and activation primitives. Initialization uses ramped half-and-half (depth 1–3) with tournament selection, crossover (0.8), and mutation (0.1). Candidate fitness is calculated by appending the synthesized feature to the base matrix and measuring held-out XGBoost validation performance (ROC-AUC or Accuracy), penalized by a complexity term (λ=0.01) and tree size. Evolution halts upon stagnation or minimal fitness variance. The top-evolved expressions are then concatenated to the original features to train the final tagging classifier, enabling direct inspection of the learned interactions.

## Key Results
- Improved MTG-Jamendo multilabel ROC-AUC by 0.002–0.005 (0.729–0.730 vs. baseline 0.727–0.719).
- Increased GTZAN multiclass accuracy by 0.035–0.050 (0.800–0.805 vs. baseline 0.765–0.740).
- Gains converged rapidly, with most performance improvements captured within the first 300–1000 GP evaluations under modest computational budgets.

## Why This Works (Mechanism)

### Mechanism 1
- Claim: Evolving explicit mathematical combinations of base descriptors can uncover non-linear, task-aligned synergies that static feature sets miss.
- Mechanism: GP conducts symbolic regression over a constrained operator library. By evaluating each candidate's impact on an XGBoost scorer, the pipeline treats feature construction as a direct optimization problem rather than heuristic fusion. Assumption: Optimal tag mappings reside in low-depth symbolic manifolds rather than requiring deep hierarchical representations.
- Evidence anchors: [abstract] "evolves composite features by mathematically combining base music features, thereby capturing synergistic interactions"; [section 2.2] fitness function evaluates candidates by augmenting the feature set and measuring XGBoost validation performance. Break condition: Symbolic composition cannot manufacture missing information if base descriptors lack perceptual or harmonic resolution relative to the target label.

### Mechanism 2
- Claim: Structural complexity constraints stabilize learning and preserve transparency without degrading predictive utility.
- Mechanism: A static tree height limit (depth ≤6) and a per-node parsimony penalty (λ=0.01) prune the evolutionary search. This actively rewards compact formulas, preventing bloat and ensuring deployed expressions remain human-readable while maintaining fitness-driven selection. Assumption: High utility in MIR tasks correlates with simpler mathematical structures; excessive depth primarily introduces overfitting.
- Evidence anchors: [abstract] top evolved expressions include low-complexity solutions aligned with parsimony pressure; [section 2.2] penalty promotes interpretability by favoring simpler expressions over complex ones. Break condition: If a tagging task inherently requires deeply contextual or multi-layer pattern recognition, parsimony pressure may artificially truncate expression depth below the information ceiling needed for accurate classification.

### Mechanism 3
- Claim: The majority of performance gains emerge early in the evolutionary run, enabling deployment under modest computational budgets.
- Mechanism: Ramped half-and-half initialization paired with subtree crossover/mutation forces early generations to sample diverse topological regions. As running averages plateau, the system shifts from broad exploration to local refinement, yielding diminishing returns once accessible feature intersections are identified. Assumption: The audio-to-tag mapping contains readily discoverable low-complexity interactions that do not require exhaustive traversal.
- Evidence anchors: [abstract] most performance gains are noticed within the first few hundred GP evaluations; [section 3.3] best-so-far curves rise steeply during early generations then flatten. Break condition: If label ambiguity or annotator disagreement dominates the dataset, the fitness landscape will become rugged, potentially delaying convergence beyond the reported thresholds.

## Foundational Learning
- **Genetic Programming / Symbolic Regression**: Provides the search engine and operator algebra for deriving transparent formulaic features; dictates initialization, mutation, and fitness evaluation. Why needed here: It enables direct optimization of feature-space geometry toward predictive utility. Quick check question: How does a tree-depth parsimony penalty differ mathematically from L2 weight regularization, and why does it matter for deploying interpretable feature formulas?
- **Tree-Based Ensemble Learning (XGBoost)**: Serves as the downstream evaluator and predictor; natively handles heterogeneous, non-linear symbolic inputs and outputs feature importance for interpretability audit. Why needed here: Provides a stable, gradient-boosted foundation that can immediately leverage newly synthesized features without architectural changes. Quick check question: Why might a gradient-boosted tree ensemble outperform logistic regression when processing conditional or trigonometric composite features?
- **Music Information Retrieval Feature Abstraction**: Defines the terminal nodes (signal descriptors, harmonic/perceptual features) that GP combines; determines the semantic ceiling of discoverable interactions. Why needed here: Establishes the atomic building blocks from which higher-order perceptual mappings are synthesized. Quick check question: What functional difference exists between signal-level metrics (e.g., RMS, Spectral Flux) and ontology-grounded harmonic mappings, and how does that gap shape composite expression discovery?

## Architecture Onboarding
- Component map: Audio preprocessing (Essentia/Omnizart/MFCC regression) -> Feature standardization (zero-mean/unit-variance) -> GP evolutionary module (DEAP, fitness=XGBoost val score, parsimony penalty λ=0.01) -> Tagging classifier (XGBoost with augmented features) -> Post-hoc co-occurrence & expression analysis.
- Critical path: Standardize base features -> Initialize GP population (ramped half-and-half, depth 1–3) -> Evaluate candidate -> Train XGBoost on augmented set -> Apply parsimony pruning -> Early-stop criteria (15-gen stagnation or fitness variance <0.0001) -> Export top expressions for inference.
- Design tradeoffs: Training-only compute latency vs. deployment simplicity; rich operator sets (trig/conditional) vs. expression bloat; early-stopping aggressiveness vs. marginal gain capture.
- Failure signatures: Persistent NaN/inf in symbolic trees, fitness variance stagnation across generations, baseline XGBoost accuracy plateaus without augmentation, GP defaulting to constant functions instead of feature combinations.
- First 3 experiments: 1. Reproduce baseline XGBoost on ALL62 and E23 using identical hyperparameters and train/val/test splits to establish 95% CI bounds and validation distribution. 2. Sweep GP population sizes (50, 100, 500) with standardized convergence logging to map evaluation-to-gains trajectory and identify budget ceilings across datasets. 3. Swap terminal feature sets (e.g., inject rhythmic or harmonic descriptors) and analyze top-50 operator-feature co-occurrence matrices to verify whether evolved interactions align with domain-specific perceptual hypotheses.

## Open Questions the Paper Calls Out
1. Can GP-evolved composite features mitigate dataset biases and spurious correlations compared to black-box models? The introduction warns that black-box models may latch onto spurious correlations, yet evaluations focus purely on predictive metrics without auditing reasoning for confounders. Resolving this requires benchmarking on datasets with known spurious features and comparing out-of-distribution robustness and feature attribution patterns against deep fusion baselines.
2. How does the evolutionary search complexity and convergence speed scale with dataset size and label cardinality? While gains appear early, testing only two datasets limits understanding of scalability. Scaling studies across varied sizes and cardinalities, tracking evaluation counts and fitness variance, are needed to map the search complexity curve.
3. Will constraining the GP operator set with music-theoretic priors improve sample efficiency and generalization? The search uses generic primitives, applying music theory only post-hoc. Ablation studies comparing standard versus music-informed operator sets would clarify whether domain-specific constraints yield faster convergence and more compact formulas.

## Limitations
- Reported performance gains depend on unspecified train/val/test splits and unconstrained XGBoost hyperparameters for the expanded feature space, raising concerns about validation overfitting.
- The symbolic search inherits a hard information ceiling; if base descriptors lack task-relevant harmonic or perceptual resolution, GP cannot synthesize missing signal content.
- External validation remains sparse within modern MIR literature, with minimal peer benchmarks or ablation studies for GP-driven feature synthesis at scale.

## Confidence
